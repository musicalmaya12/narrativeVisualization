Conclusion

What can we gather from these scatterplots?
Based on the direction of the dots for all three countries, there is a logarithmic positive correlation between price and ranking,
i.e., the greater the price, the higher the ranking, but after a certain threshold price for each country, rankings plateau.

Italy:
  • The most expensive wine is also the highest ranked.
  • Nebbiolo wines tend to have both the lowest and highest rankings.
  • The cheapest wine is also the lowest ranked (Pinot Noir).
France:
  • Both the most expensive and highest ranked wines are of variety Chardonnay, but from different wineries.
  • The lowest ranked wine is a Zinfandel.
  • The cheapest wine is also a Chardonnay.
The United States:
  • The most expensive wines are Cabernet Sauvignons in the US.
  • The lowest ranked wine is a Zinfandel, just like France.
  • The cheapest wine is a Viognier.
